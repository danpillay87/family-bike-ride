An idea, for you 💚

One Big Adventure, One Family Finish

I do the long, hard miles solo. You and the boys meet me. Then we all cross the line together — pick where that line is.

How it works (whichever we pick)

The hard bit's mine. The lovely bit's ours.

Day 1 — me

I ride solo & camp 🏕️

I take on the big mileage alone, at my own pace, and rough it for one night. You get a quiet evening at home.

Day 2 — us

You & the boys join 🚗➡️🚲

You drive down fresh, meet me partway, and we do a gentle flat stretch together to a comfy night, all four of us.

Day 3 — finish

Over the line together 🏁

A short, scenic roll to the finish — all of us across it as a family. Then home in the car.

The choice is yours

Three finishes — here's the full picture

🧀 Cheddar Gorge My lean
A jaw-dropping gorge finish — and the gentlest family ride of the three.
🚗 ~2 hrs to Somerset🚲 ~10 flat miles for us🛤️ traffic-free🏁 the gorge
My solo bit
I ride out of Bath along the famous Bristol & Bath railway path and on toward the Mendips — roughly 40 miles — and camp near Cheddar.
You join at
Yatton or Axbridge — easy to drive to, park up and unload the bikes.
Our ride together
The Strawberry Line: ~10 miles of flat, traffic-free old railway through orchards and countryside. No hills at all.
The finish
Rolling straight into Cheddar Gorge — towering cliffs, caves and the cheese.
Boys will love
Caves, ice cream, real Cheddar cheese, and a flat path they can pedal bits of themselves.
We'd sleep
A family pub or glamping near Cheddar / Axbridge.
There & home
~2 hrs' drive to Somerset. You've got the car, so home's sorted — handy, as Cheddar has no train station.
💛 Why it's my lean: I get a proper big ride, you get the easiest family leg, and the boys get the most spectacular finish.
🏖️ The Sea From our front door
No driving to a start line — I roll out from home; you join for the run to the beach.
🚗 nothing to get to🚲 ~12 flat miles for us🛤️ traffic-free🏁 the beach
My solo bit
I ride straight from our door down the Downs Link, an old railway line heading for the coast — roughly 30 miles — and camp near Henfield.
You join at
Southwater, near Horsham — a short drive with easy parking.
Our ride together
Flat, traffic-free old railway down to the sea — ~12 gentle miles through fields and woods.
The finish
Shoreham beach — paddling, stone-skimming and chips. (We could carry on to Brighton if we're flying.)
Boys will love
Southwater's dinosaur playground 🦕, the ruined Bramber Castle to clamber on, then the beach.
We'd sleep
Glamping or a village B&B around Henfield / Partridge Green.
There & home
Nothing to get to — it starts at home. You drive down to join, and the car brings us all back from the coast.
💛 The trade-off: closest and easiest of the three — but my solo day is the flattest, so least of a "challenge" for me (I'll cope 😄).
⛪ Canterbury Cathedral + coast
The romantic one — the ancient Pilgrims' Way, a cathedral, and oysters by the sea.
🚗 ~2 hrs to Kent🚲 ~8 + 8 gentle miles for us🛤️ mostly traffic-free🏁 cathedral + sea
My solo bit
I ride a real stretch of the ancient Pilgrims' Way across the Kent hills — roughly 50 miles, the proper adventure — and camp en route.
You join at
Chilham — a gorgeous medieval village with a castle, square and tea room. Worth the trip on its own.
Our ride together
Gentle riverside path and quiet lanes down the Stour valley into Canterbury (~8 miles), then the Crab & Winkle Way to the sea (~8 miles).
The finish
Canterbury Cathedral first, then a victory roll down to oysters and the beach at Whitstable.
Boys will love
Chilham castle, the giant cathedral, and fish & chips on Whitstable seafront 🦪.
We'd sleep
The village pub at Chilham, or in Canterbury itself.
There & home
~2 hrs to Kent by car or train; stations at Wye, Canterbury and Whitstable make it easy.
💛 Why pick it: two finishes in one — a cathedral and the sea — plus storybook villages. The furthest to get to, though.
